## Overview

Scout Finch grows up in Depression-era Alabama as her father, attorney Atticus Finch, defends a Black man falsely accused of a crime. The novel weaves childhood innocence with the harsh realities of racial injustice in the American South.

## Memorable Quotes

> "You never really understand a person until you consider things from his point of view... Until you climb inside of his skin and walk around in it."

> "The one thing that doesn't abide by majority rule is a person's conscience."

- Loss of innocence
