## Overview

Harari traces the history of humankind from the Stone Age to the present, arguing that Homo sapiens conquered the world thanks to our unique ability to believe in shared fictions — from religion and money to nations and human rights.

## Memorable Quotes

> "You could never convince a monkey to give you a banana by promising him limitless bananas after death in monkey heaven."

> "How do you cause people to believe in an imagined order such as Christianity, democracy or capitalism? First, you never admit that the order is imagined."

## Themes

- The cognitive revolution and shared myths
- The agricultural revolution as history's biggest fraud
- The unification of humankind through money, empires, and religion
- The scientific revolution and the future of our species
